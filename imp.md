There is no single "turnkey" open-source tool like InVideo AI that automatically pulls stock images, writes scripts, creates voiceovers, and edits a timeline for you. True open-source tools require technical setup and do not have "faceless video builder" automation built out-of-the-box.However, you can achieve a highly capable, free workflow using the following open-source and free AI options:1. Scriptwriting & VoiceoverText Generation: Use OpenAI ChatGPT or Google AI Studio to generate your video script and scene-by-scene prompts.Voiceover: Use local open-source tools like Coqui TTS or Bark, or utilize generous free tiers on platforms like ElevenLabs for automated, realistic voiceovers.2. Open-Source AI Video & Image GenerationInstead of stock photos, you can generate video clips directly from your script text.Top Models: HunyuanVideo (Tencent), Mochi 1 (Genmo), and Wan 2.2 are among the most powerful open-source video models.Getting Started Easily: If you have a PC with a dedicated Nvidia GPU, you can use Pinocchio. It provides one-click installations for running these powerful open-source AI video models locally.3. Alternative "Done-For-You" Faceless ToolsIf you want the automated convenience of InVideo AI but want to avoid its watermark or credit limitations, consider these freemium and cost-effective alternatives:Pictory AI: Best for automatically turning blog posts and scripts into summarized videos using stock footage and AI voiceovers.VEED.IO: Offers robust automated AI tools for text-to-video, subtitles, and character generators within a web browser.HeyGen: A popular tool for scaling faceless social media channels, generating human-like avatars, and synthesizing audio.
